UX Research Approach Statement

JP Carrascal
I approach research through shared ownership. I start by working in close collaboration with stakeholders (designers, product managers, engineers) to develop a crisp set of hypotheses and research questions, in order to have a shared understanding of the problem. That initial alignment allows the team to decide on the best research method to tackle the problem, guided by UXR expertise. Alignment is also necessary to craft the required study materials, from study guides to prototypes.

When running studies, hands-on involvement from stakeholders is key. I believe the best learning experience comes from direct interaction with customers. Countless times I've seen product teams get inspired to take action and improve the product after talking to customers or seeing them running into issues while using the product during observational studies.

Solid research insights, models and frameworks drive design decisions. Collaboration during the synthesis phase allows the team to build confidence, objectivity and ownership. This ensures that results are grounded in customer, product and business needs, and that teams adopt frameworks and models.
